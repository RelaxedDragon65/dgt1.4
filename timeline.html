    • 1900

    The first computer, known as the "Analytical Engine," was designed by Charles Babbage in the mid-19th century. However, it was never fully built, and its designs were only partially implemented in later machines.

    1930s

    The first electronic digital computer, the Atanasoff-Berry Computer, was built in the late 1930s by John Atanasoff and Clifford Berry. It used binary digits and capacitors to store data.

    world war 2

    During World War II, the first programmable computer, the Colossus, was built by the British to decode encrypted messages sent by the Germans. It was an important tool in the Allied victory.

    1950s

    In the 1950s, the first commercial computer, the UNIVAC, was introduced. It was used for scientific and business purposes and was a major milestone in the development of modern computing.

    1970s

    The invention of the microprocessor in the early 1970s marked a major breakthrough in computer technology. It allowed for the creation of smaller, more powerful computers that could be used by individuals.

    1980s

    In the 1980s, personal computers became more widely available and affordable. Companies like Apple and IBM introduced computers that were easy to use and had a graphical user interface.

    1990s

    The development of the Internet in the 1990s changed the way people communicate and share information. It allowed for global connectivity and led to the creation of new industries and businesses.

    2000s

    The 2000s saw the rise of mobile computing, with the introduction of smartphones and tablets. These devices have transformed the way people access information and interact with technology.

    Our Time

    In recent years, artificial intelligence and machine learning have become increasingly important in the development of new technologies. These fields have the potential to revolutionize many industries, from healthcare to transportation.

    The future

    Looking to the future, quantum computing is an area of research that could lead to major breakthroughs in computing power and speed. It is a rapidly evolving field that holds great promise for the future of computing.
